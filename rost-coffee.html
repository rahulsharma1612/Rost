The Craft

The ROST Journey

At ROST, we view coffee as a temporal art form. Our process begins with micro-lot beans, hand-selected for their unique terroir.

We employ a slow-immersion cold brew technique, allowing the beans to steep for 24 hours in temperature-controlled environments. This patient extraction yields a velvet-smooth profile, devoid of bitterness, highlighting the delicate floral and fruity notes that high-temperature brewing often destroys.

Our Story

Born on a Mountain. Brewed for the Modern World.

ROST.coffee began on a winter morning at 11,152 feet on Hatu Peak. Three friends, one battered thermos, and the kind of coffee that makes you understand why people climb mountains in the first place.

We started ROST because we couldn't find a cold brew in India that respected the bean. Most were burnt, bitter, or built for shelf life — not for taste. So we built our own.

Good coffee is patient. We learned that the hard way.

What We Believe

Three Principles. Zero Compromise.

The rules we made for ourselves on day one — and still follow.

eco

Traceable to the Tree

Every bean we use is single-origin and farm-identified. No blends of mystery, no shortcuts. You should know where your coffee grew up.

schedule

Slow Is the Point

Our cold brew steeps for 24 hours. Our roast batches are small. Our shipping is direct. Patience isn't a marketing word — it's the recipe.

handshake

Fair on Every Hand

We pay farmers above market, we keep our team small and paid well, and we never use a packaging supplier we wouldn't visit ourselves.

The Timeline

How We Got Here

Four years. Three founders. One very long brewing experiment.

2021 · The Spark

A Thermos at 11,152 Feet

On a trip to Hatu Peak in Himachal Pradesh, the three of us drank the worst cold brew of our lives — and decided we'd build a better one. We started brewing in a small kitchen in Gurugram that weekend.

2022 · The Test Kitchen

200 Batches. 1 Recipe.

We spent a year testing extraction times, bean origins, and bottle formats. Friends became unwilling taste-testers. We threw out more than we kept.

2023 · The First Pour

First 500 Bottles

Our first batch sold out in 11 days, mostly to people we knew. We took the feedback, tweaked the roast, and built a small roastery outside Delhi.

2024 · The Launch

ROST Goes Live

Today, we ship across India, partner with three single-origin farms, and run a subscription that lets people taste a different lot every month. We're just getting started.
